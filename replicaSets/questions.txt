CKA: REPLICASETS — QUESTIONS & ANSWERS (Deeper Understanding)
============================================================

FOUNDATION
----------

1) Q: What is a ReplicaSet?
   A: A controller that ensures a specific number of Pod replicas are running at all times.

2) Q: Why do we need ReplicaSets?
   A: For self-healing and scaling: if a Pod dies, RS recreates it; if too many exist, RS removes extras.

3) Q: Does a ReplicaSet schedule Pods onto nodes?
   A: No. The scheduler decides node placement. RS only creates Pod objects.

4) Q: Does a ReplicaSet talk directly to etcd?
   A: No. It uses kube-apiserver. The API server persists state in etcd.

LABELS + SELECTORS (MOST IMPORTANT)
-----------------------------------

5) Q: How does a ReplicaSet know which pods to manage?
   A: Using its selector (spec.selector). It manages pods whose labels match the selector.

6) Q: What is the difference between spec.selector and spec.template.labels?
   A:
   - spec.selector: defines which pods the RS will count/manage.
   - spec.template.labels: labels applied to new pods created by RS.
   They should match.

7) Q: What happens if RS selector and template labels do NOT match?
   A: The RS may create pods but not manage/count them correctly, or it may manage other pods
      that match the selector unexpectedly. This can break desired replica behavior.

8) Q: Can a ReplicaSet manage pods you created manually?
   A: Yes, if those pods match its selector, RS will adopt/manage them.

DEPLOYMENT RELATIONSHIP
-----------------------

9) Q: What is the relationship between Deployment and ReplicaSet?
   A: Deployment manages ReplicaSets; ReplicaSets manage Pods.

10) Q: Why do Deployments use ReplicaSets?
    A: To support rolling updates and rollbacks by creating new RS revisions and scaling
       old/new RS up/down.

11) Q: After updating a Deployment image, why do you see multiple ReplicaSets?
    A: Each rollout revision creates a new ReplicaSet. Old RS stays for rollback history.

PRACTICAL / CKA COMMANDS
------------------------

12) Q: Which command is best to debug why a ReplicaSet is not creating pods?
    A: kubectl describe rs <rs-name> (check Events and selector/labels)

13) Q: How do you confirm which pods belong to a ReplicaSet?
    A:
    - kubectl describe rs <rs-name> (shows selector + pods)
    - kubectl get pods -l <label-selector>

14) Q: How do you scale a ReplicaSet?
    A: kubectl scale rs <rs-name> --replicas=<N>
       (In real apps, scaling the Deployment is preferred.)

15) Q: How do you find the owner (who created the RS)?
    A:
    - kubectl get rs <rs> -o yaml | look for ownerReferences
    - If ownerReferences.kind=Deployment, the RS is controlled by a Deployment.

TROUBLESHOOTING PATTERNS
------------------------

16) Q: RS desired replicas is 3 but current is 0. What are top reasons?
    A:
    - ImagePullBackOff / ErrImagePull
    - FailedScheduling (resource/taints/affinity)
    - Namespace quotas / limit ranges
    - Missing permissions (rare in standard clusters)
    Check: Events in describe output.

17) Q: RS has pods but Service has no endpoints. How can this happen?
    A: Pods may be not Ready (readinessProbe failing) OR Service selector doesn't match
       pod labels. RS being healthy does not guarantee Service routing.

18) Q: Pod keeps coming back after you delete it. Why?
    A: The ReplicaSet controller recreates it to maintain desired replicas.

19) Q: In one line, how do you differentiate Deployment vs ReplicaSet in a real cluster?
    A: Deployment manages rollouts/rollbacks and creates ReplicaSets; ReplicaSet only maintains
       a stable pod replica count.

EXAM SUMMARY
------------

20) Q: What are the 3 key things to remember for CKA about ReplicaSets?
    A:
    - RS maintains desired pod count (self-healing).
    - RS uses labels/selectors to manage pods.
    - Deployments create/manage RS for rolling updates and rollbacks.
