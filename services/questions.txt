CKA: SERVICES — QUESTIONS & ANSWERS (Deep Understanding)
=======================================================

FOUNDATION
----------

1) Q: What is a Service in Kubernetes?
   A: A stable networking abstraction that provides access to a dynamic set of Pods.

2) Q: Why do we need a Service if Pods already have IPs?
   A: Pod IPs can change when Pods restart or get recreated. A Service gives a stable IP and DNS name.

3) Q: What is the default Service type?
   A: ClusterIP.

4) Q: What does a ClusterIP Service do?
   A: It exposes the application only inside the cluster.

SELECTORS + ENDPOINTS
---------------------

5) Q: How does a Service know which Pods to send traffic to?
   A: By using a selector that matches Pod labels.

6) Q: What happens if the Service selector does not match any Pods?
   A: The Service will have no endpoints and traffic will not reach any backend Pods.

7) Q: What are endpoints in a Service?
   A: The real backend Pod IPs and ports selected by the Service.

8) Q: How do you check whether a Service has backends?
   A: kubectl get endpoints <service-name>

9) Q: Why can a Service exist but still not work?
   A: Because it may have no endpoints, wrong selector, non-ready Pods, or wrong targetPort.

PORTS
-----

10) Q: What is the difference between port and targetPort?
    A:
    - port = the port exposed by the Service
    - targetPort = the port on the backend Pod/container

11) Q: Give an example of port mapping.
    A:
    - Service port 80
    - targetPort 8080
    Means traffic to Service:80 goes to Pod:8080

SERVICE TYPES
-------------

12) Q: What is NodePort?
    A: A Service type that exposes the app on a port on every node.

13) Q: How is a NodePort Service accessed?
    A: Using NodeIP:NodePort

14) Q: What is LoadBalancer Service used for?
    A: To expose an application externally through a cloud provider load balancer.

15) Q: Which Service type is most commonly used for internal microservice communication?
    A: ClusterIP.

NAMESPACES + DNS
----------------

16) Q: Are Services namespaced?
    A: Yes.

17) Q: Can a Service in namespace prod select Pods in namespace dev?
    A: No. A Service only selects Pods in the same namespace.

18) Q: How do you access a Service in another namespace?
    A: Use:
      service-name.namespace
    Example:
      nginx-service.prod

19) Q: What is the full DNS format of a Service?
    A: service-name.namespace.svc.cluster.local

PRACTICAL / CKA
---------------

20) Q: Which command is best to inspect selector, ports, endpoints, and type of a Service?
    A: kubectl describe svc <service-name>

21) Q: How do you create a Service quickly from an existing Pod?
    A: kubectl expose pod <pod-name> --port=80 --name=<service-name>

22) Q: How do you test a ClusterIP Service from your laptop without changing Service type?
    A: Use port-forward:
      kubectl port-forward svc/<service-name> 8080:80

23) Q: If a Service is correct but the app still fails, what should you check next?
    A:
    - Pod labels
    - Pod readiness
    - endpoints
    - container port / targetPort
    - kube-proxy or networking if needed

DEEPER UNDERSTANDING
--------------------

24) Q: What is the difference between a Pod and a Service?
    A:
    - Pod runs the actual container/application
    - Service provides stable access to one or more Pods

25) Q: Does a Service run containers?
    A: No. It is only a networking abstraction.

26) Q: Why is readiness important for Services?
    A: Services should send traffic only to healthy/ready Pods. If Pods are not Ready, endpoints may be missing.

27) Q: In one line, how would you explain Services in an interview?
    A: A Kubernetes Service provides stable network access to a dynamic group of Pods using labels/selectors and supports internal or external exposure through types like ClusterIP, NodePort, and LoadBalancer.

CKA QUICK CHECKLIST
-------------------
- Service not working?
  1) kubectl describe svc <name>
  2) kubectl get endpoints <name>
  3) kubectl get pods --show-labels
  4) check targetPort/containerPort
  5) check namespace
