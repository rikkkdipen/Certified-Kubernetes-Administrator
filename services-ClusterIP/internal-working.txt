You create a Service, and Kubernetes gives it a virtual internal IP

The Service uses its selector to find matching Pods

Kubernetes keeps a backend list of those Pods as endpoints

When traffic hits the ClusterIP, kube-proxy on the node uses iptables/IPVS rules to forward that traffic to one of the backend Pod IPs

So the ClusterIP itself is not a real Pod — it’s a stable virtual address that routes traffic to changing Pods behind it.
